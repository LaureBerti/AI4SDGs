## Introduction

GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks) are deep neural architectures where two networksâ€”a generator and a discriminatorâ€”compete, enabling realistic synthetic data creation. In healthcare, where labeled medical data are scarce, expensive, and privacy-sensitive, GANs can generate realistic images (e.g., X-rays, MRIs), time-series, and electronic health records (EHR), boosting model performance. While GAN-generated data offer potential to improve diagnostics, training robustness, and equity, they require careful validation to avoid clinical misinterpretation and protect patient privacy.

## Conclusion

GAN-based augmentation of healthcare data is a transformative force, addressing data scarcity while supporting improved model accuracy and equity. However, clinical validity, privacy, and bias must be carefully managed. The future lies in **differentially private GANs, federated model training**, and **multimodal data synthesis** (combining imaging, vitals, and genomics). Collaborative efforts among clinicians, data scientists, ethicists, and regulators will be essential to build trustworthy, impactful solutions.
